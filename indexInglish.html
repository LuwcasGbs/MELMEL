How to get your first job?

The theme “First Job” can cover fundamental topics such as the importance of the first job for personal and professional development, as well as practical guidelines for preparing for it. This includes tips for creating an attractive resume and portfolio, strategies for seeking opportunities, and preparing for interviews and selection processes. It can also explore ethics and behavior in the workplace, how to stand out, deal with feedback, and manage initial challenges. For the technology area, it is relevant to discuss entry-level roles, technical skills in demand, and share practical experiences. Finally, the theme can include reflections on learning and planning for career growth.

Legislation

Art. 403. Any work by minors under sixteen years of age is prohibited, except as an apprentice, from the age of fourteen." (NR)

Art. 403 of the CLT prohibits the work of minors under 16 years of age, except as apprentices from the age of 14. The rule protects the physical, psychological and social development of young people, aligning with the ECA and international standards, guaranteeing access to adequate education and training.

What types of work can I do?

For high school students, the Young Apprentice program is ideal. After college, analyst positions are best suited for recent graduates. Starting a career can be challenging, but there are several initial options in the job market:

  • Administrative Assistant: Organizes agendas, meetings and assists in the strategic management of the company.

  • Receptionist: Represents the company, manages communication flows and connects people.

  • Telemarketing: Works in customer service, listening, solving problems and selling.

  • Salesperson: Creates connections with customers and conveys passion for the products.

  • Office Boy/Girl: Performs external tasks, such as deliveries and payments, ensuring efficiency.

  • Supermarket Cashier: Handles transactions and direct customer service.

  • Stockkeeper: Organizes stock and ensures the flow of materials.

  • Store Attendant: Organizes shelves and serves customers, with potential for growth.

  • Waiter/Waitress: Serves customers in restaurants, focusing on efficiency and courtesy.

  • Cleaning Assistant: Keeps environments organized, with high demand in various sectors.
