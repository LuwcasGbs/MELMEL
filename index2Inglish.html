Technology and AI

Technology in the job market is a resource increasingly used by companies. After the restrictions imposed by the Covid-19 pandemic, the use of modern systems has become essential for all organizations that wish to serve their customers with the quality and urgency that the market demands. We have some points of technological advancement in the world of work: structural unemployment; inequality; social isolation; risk to mental health; technological dependence; excessive exploitation of labor, among others.

Technology in companies is crucial for several reasons. It automates activities, optimizes them and boosts employee efficiency. Another important point is that, in a competitive market, innovative and agile companies perform better by meeting demands quickly.

Artificial intelligence is important in the job market because it is transforming the way companies operate and creating new opportunities. AI helps improve the efficiency and productivity of operations, in addition to allowing them to make more informed decisions based on data and analysis.

Impact of Artificial Intelligence on companies

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is transforming the corporate landscape by providing: Improved decision-making: By analyzing large volumes of data, AI generates insights that improve business strategies and decisions in areas such as HR, where it facilitates hiring aligned with the corporate culture. Operational transformation: Automates repetitive tasks, increasing efficiency, reducing errors and freeing up human resources for more strategic and innovative activities. Customer personalization and experience: Enables highly personalized marketing and service, improving customer satisfaction and loyalty. Improved security: Identifies and prevents cyber and physical threats with intelligent algorithms. Sustainability and energy efficiency: Optimizes resources and reduces waste, promoting more sustainable practices. Skills improvement and job creation: Creates new job categories and demand for specialization, such as data analysis, while AI-based platforms offer continuous and personalized training. Improved working conditions: Replaces humans in dangerous tasks, improving safety in the workplace. While AI changes the nature of jobs, it also opens up opportunities for innovation and growth, highlighting its role as a transformative force in business.

Challenges

Artificial intelligence (AI) affects some of the current jobs, but it also opens up opportunities for workers and entrepreneurs, according to experts gathered this Tuesday (9) at the Chamber of Deputies. They participated in the debate “Professions at Risk: Artificial Intelligence and Employability”. Deputy Helio Lopes (PL-RJ), rapporteur of an ongoing study at the Center for Strategic Studies and Debates (Cedes), coordinated the meeting. “The net result of the impacts of artificial intelligence is uncertain, it will depend on companies and workers”, summarized Marcello Luiz de Souza Junior, one of the managers in São Paulo of the National Industrial Training Service (Senai). The research indicates that 37% of jobs in Brazil are exposed to generative artificial intelligence, which means that this portion may undergo significant changes in the way they work.

Relationship between Technology and First Job

Technology has a significant impact on first jobs, expanding opportunities and requiring new skills.

  • Easy access: Digital platforms and social networks help young people find jobs.

  • Emerging sectors: Areas such as IT, digital marketing and the sharing economy offer accessible jobs.

  • Digital skills: Basic tools and technological knowledge are essential even in traditional jobs.

  • Online training: Free courses and institutions such as SENAI prepare young people for the technology market.

  • Challenges and opportunities: While technology increases competition, it also promotes innovation, automation and remote work.

Technology not only expands opportunities for first jobs, but also defines many of the skills needed to enter the market. Young people who quickly adapt to tools and innovations have a better chance of success, especially in a scenario where digital transformation is constant. Technological training, therefore, is an indispensable differentiator.

Humanization of technology

The humanization of Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the development of technologies that mimic human behaviors, such as communication, decision-making, and emotional interaction. The goal is to make interactions with AI more natural, intuitive, and effective for users, impacting employment by creating new demands for human skills, such as creativity and empathy, and by improving human-machine interaction at work. New opportunities: Professions in areas such as AI training, experience design, and hybrid customer service are on the rise. Support for workers: Humanized AIs automate repetitive tasks, facilitate communication, and offer personalized training.

The humanization of technology is an approach that seeks to ensure that technology is at the service of people, not the other way around, such as including differences in management and care processes. Such changes are built not by an isolated person or group, but in a collective and shared way. Inclusion to stimulate the production of new ways of caring and new ways of organizing work. With telemedicine, for example, the team of specialist doctors who would otherwise only be responsible for evaluating exams can be used to provide care. The team is thus reinforced by specialists who can provide support and reduce the number of daily consultations made by each professional. Technological innovations, such as virtual assistants and IoT (Internet of Things) devices, are transforming people's daily lives, providing greater convenience and efficiency. Virtual assistants, such as Alexa and Google Assistant, simplify tasks through intuitive voice commands.
